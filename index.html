The initial strike of the
Great Depression

The depression came about through political misguidance and economic policies. It struck the millions of investors within Wall Street and not soon after, the many people within industrial jobs.

The majority of Americans
who were struck by the depression

The unemployed populous had to rely on bread lines for food since they could not buy their own food due to the lack of present jobs during the period.

Presidency during the economic downturn of
the Great Depression

President Herbert Hoover had been the president through the first 3 years of the depression. However, Hoover lost the election of '32 to Franklin Delano Roosevelt

Small snippets and facts of the Great Depression

Pieces of evidence and statistics of the Great Depression
that will broaden the perspective of impact the occurrence had

    The beginning and end of the Great Depression

    The stock market crashed on "Black Tuesday" (Oct. 29, 1929) and ended after 10 years (1939)

    The percentage of unemployment within the United States

    At its peak, 25% of the American population was unemployed which was roughly 12 million people. Each day, over 12,000 people became unemployed

    Catalysts for the Great Depression

    Stock Market Crash, Bank failures, lack of credit, bankruptcies, unemployment, reduction in purchasing, American economic policy and failures by the Federal Reserve, loss of exports, drought conditions and the Dust Bowl.

    Hoover's failed lie

    Hoover made this statement to avoid public notice of the downturning economy,
    "The fundamental business of the country, that is, production and distribution of commodities, is on a sound and prosperous basis.."

Conflicts arise within the United States

With a sudden drop in economy and political grasp being at an all-time low, jobs were scarce to find and acute deflation took part amongst the world.
